Full Description

Burma Teak (Genuine Teak) is a world-renowned tropical hardwood prized for its remarkable combination of beauty, natural oils, high dimensional stability and outstanding resistance to rot, insects and water. Harvested from mature trees across Myanmar and neighboring regions, this wood is the first choice for high-end furniture, exterior decking, boat-building, and architectural joinery. The heartwood varies from golden-brown to deep amber and darkens over time to a rich patina when exposed to sunlight.

Why Burma Teak is special

  • Natural oils: Gives exceptional resistance against decay, marine borers and termites — often used un-coated outdoors.
  • Workability: Machine and hand work very well — sands to a fine smooth finish; glues and screws hold excellently.
  • Low shrinkage: Dimensionally stable across humidity changes — ideal for doors, windows and boat decks.
  • Longevity: Can last 50–100+ years with minimal maintenance in exterior use when properly installed.

Common Applications

Exterior Furniture, Luxury Indoor Furniture, Yacht & Boat Building, Decking, Outdoor Joinery, Window/Door Frames, High-end Flooring, Carvings and Specialty Millwork.

Technical Specs & Handling

Average Dry Density 650 – 750 kg / m³
Typical Moisture Content (kiln-dried) 8% – 12%
Janka Hardness (approx.) ~1,070 lbf
Workability Notes Easy to saw & shape; pre-drill for screws if required; oils may affect glue — clean surfaces before gluing.
Finishing Sands to smooth finish; shows beautiful sheen with oils or varnish. Natural oil finishes enhance water resistance.

Origin & Sustainability

Traditionally sourced from Myanmar (Burma). Note: due to environmental and legal concerns in some regions, always ask your supplier for chain-of-custody documentation or FSC / legally-sourced certification when sustainability or legality is a requirement.

Care & Maintenance

  • For outdoor use, apply teak oil or UV-protective finishes periodically to slow greying and maintain color.
  • Clean gently with mild detergent and soft brush; avoid high-pressure washing that damages fibers.
  • Store dry and elevated from ground before installation to prevent moisture pickup.
