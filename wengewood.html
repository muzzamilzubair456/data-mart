Exotic African Darkwood

Wenge Wood

Nature’s most dramatic hardwood. Wenge is a rare African timber celebrated for its near-black espresso color and striking "partridge" grain patterns—perfect for high-end modern interiors.

Hardness

~1,630 lbf Janka

Density

~880 kg/m³

Modern
Artistry

Characteristics

Wenge is an exceptionally dense and heavy wood. When finished, it reaches a darkness that rivals Ebony, but with a unique texture of alternating light and dark streaks that add incredible depth to any surface.

  • Deep Chocolate to Black Tone
  • High-Contrast Grain Texture
  • Extremely Heavy and Durable
  • Naturally Resistant to Decay
  • Premium Modern Aesthetic
  • High Dimensional Stability

Common Applications

  • Contemporary Furniture
  • Luxury Wood Flooring
  • Statement Kitchen Cabinets
  • Custom Staircase Treads
  • Musical Instruments (Fretboards)
  • Architectural Panelwork

Care & Handling

Origin: Tropical Africa (Central Africa).

Handling: Wenge is very hard and requires sharp carbide tools for machining. It responds best to clear oil finishes that deepen its natural darkness without hiding the grain. Due to its density, it provides a very solid feel in any construction.
